Page 1

In 1954, the "Joey" was constructed in a small garage at Torrens Park
to my original design for a small sailplane to suit my small self.


Page 2


Page 3

The great day was on Jan 11th 1955.
Ted Pascoe lent his trailer and his father, the Chev. Ron Adair helped with the cable.
Three short hops were made in a stiff breeze in the late afternoon at Gawler.


Page 4

The wonderful little Fiat tried hard but the glider slowed her down too much
on the launch, so we had to get a Holden. Sirley quickly became expert in the
art of launching. Lynn held the wing tip on takeoff and we needed no other help.


Page 5

Among those who have flown the Joey are Ted Pascoe, Kev Sedgman,
Brian Creer, Bob Rowe, Dud Schultz, Merv Waghorn, Cliff Gurr,
Geoff Horwood, Roger Mann (Eng), Reg Harris, Harry Schnieder.


Page 6

One of the advantages of the Joey is the ease with which it can be trailered
and at practically no expense. Launching is no problem - such as off a road
(see top left at Horrocks Pass), off the beach, a paddock or off the top of
Sellicks Hill (by hand). Some of the places we have flown from are Gawler,
Parafield, Waikeriie, Clare, Burra, Sellicks Hill and Beach, Blanchetown,
Pt. Pirie, Pt. Augusta West, Mallala.


Page 7

Kev Sedgman and I entered the Joey in the State Championships at Waikerie Xmas 54. Doug Nicholson crewed for us and we were regarded at long odds. Opinions changed however when Kev cleaned up the field in a race from Waikerie to Morgan and return on the first contest day. Two days later he made a goal flight to Tailem Bend. I failed miserably but my chance came in February when I won my 'Silver C' with my first two cross country flights (See "Best flights" above).


Page 8

During the winter of 56 I rebuilt the nose and Canopy and made numerour other alterations which gave more cockpit room and a better performance at high speeds. A proof load equivalent to 5gs was also applied to the wing.

Page 11

Kevin Sedgman built this sailplane to my revised design of the Joey. He named this particular 'J2' the "Jumbuck". Kev is noted for his performances and aerobatics in it. K Sedgman flew his "Jumbuck" in the 57/58 competitions at Gawler and came second in the Team championship and in the Pilots average. Goal flight Gawler to Peterboro.
